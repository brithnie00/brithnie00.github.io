Durian Fruit Growth Dynamics

In Agro Product Technology, mastering the growth kinetics of elite cultivars like Musang King (D197) is crucial. By quantifying physical biomass accumulation over Days After Fruit Set (DAFS), we can accurately forecast nutrient assimilation demands, estimate maturity markers, and schedule post-harvest supply chain operations.

Source Database: ResearchGate: Musang King Durian Growth Kinetics (2025/2026)

f(x) Mathematical Foundations

The cumulative weight of the developing durian is modeled using a Logistic Sigmoid Growth Model, characterizing the three natural growth phases: Lag (0–28 DAFS), Linear/Exponential (28–70 DAFS), and Stationary/Maturity (70–90 DAFS).

Cumulative Fruit Weight Model
$$f(x) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)}}$$
Growth Velocity (First Derivative)
$$f'(x) = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(x - x_0)}}{(1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)})^2}$$
Equivalent to: $$f'(x) = k \cdot f(x) \left(1 - \frac{f(x)}{L}\right)$$
x: Days After Fruit Set (DAFS)
L (Carrying Capacity): 2160 g
k (Growth Coefficient): 0.09
x₀ (Inflection Point): 49 Days
